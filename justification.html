Ideation and Navigation Flowchart

For starters, we came up with the idea of making it a one-page website. This because we had in mind that people in our target group, aged 15 to 25 years old, would want to spend as little time on a website as possible. So to have everything on one page, so that people would have to make barely any clicks, we thought would be best. We first started with a navigation flowchart keeping "everything one page" in mind, but we quickly came to the realization that we, at the very least, needed a separate page for the disclaimers necessary. Thus we ended up with the current set-up: having almost everything on one page. As seen in the navigation flowchart of the current site, the contact portion moved to a separate page (with a link included in the navigation bar) as well as making a dedicated disclaimer page. The disclaimer is accessible via a link in the footer.

Sketches and Prototypes

Following this, we came up with a prototype to follow through with this idea, as can be seen in the images. Not pictured here: the separate contact page.

Color Palettes and Fonts

We based the colors of the prototype on the color scheme present in the brand road map, later on adding another color, the green shade. This due to getting some feedback from peers that the website looked a bit too business-like and reminded them of a bank more than anything else. Adding the spring-like green helped to deviate a bit from this image. As for the font, we had trouble finding and getting the same font present in the brand road map, so instead we opted for a different font that looks similar, Open Sans.

User Testing

We, then, began user testing. In order to create the best version of what we visioned to be our website we asked test users to provide feedback on what did and did not work in regard to design, general aesthetics and usability.

About

ScreenD is a mobile application that allows you to both keep track of your passwords and figure out what other apps and websites are using your data for. When your personal information is at risk, you receive a notification on how big said risk is, and what steps to take next.

This website was created by Breda University students.
